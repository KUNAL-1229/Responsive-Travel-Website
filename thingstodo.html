1. Rafting

By the River Beas, river rafting in Kullu Manali is popular among both experienced and novice rafters. The rafting part begins in a little neighbourhood called Pirdi and passes through a number of places, including Bajaura, Sarabai, Bhuntar, Shamshi, Mohal, Raisol, and Katrain.

At Manali, there are numerous service providers who organise the activity and hire out rafts, helmets, life jackets, and oars. On request, tent lodging, campfires, and bonfires are offered. The price per person for rafting at Kullu-Manali ranges from 500 to 2000 Indian rupees.

2. Skiing

Manali, a popular tourist destination in North India, attracts skiers from across the nation because of the superb skiing conditions there. Skiing is a fantastic activity on the sweeping slopes of Rohtang Pass and Solang Valley.

They are well-liked by beginners learning their first trick while providing the best terrain for professionals skiing in Manali. In order to experience the excitement of skiing in the scenic environment of Manali, trust your skilled instructor if you enjoy adventure.

3. Scuba Diving

Scuba Diving in Havelock Islands
The best location for diving in South Andaman is on Havelock Island. You can take a 90-minute catamaran boat from Port Blair to get here, or you can take a 2.5-hour government ferry.

Diving Charges: The diving starts at 1500 for a shore dive and PADI's one day course costs around INR 4500 (You can take one dive under the course and do the remaining course of 3 more dives anywhere within one year to complete the certification)

Charges for a boat: If they take you on a boat for dive, it costs around INR 5500

4. Boating

A stunning tiny island in the North and Middle Andaman Administrative District, Baratang Island, often referred to as Ranchiwalas Island, is located around 100 kilometres from Port Blair by road. Mangroves, mud volcanoes (the only ones in India), and limestone caves are some of the island's best-known features. To explore these caves, you might need to obtain authorization from the Baratang Forest Department.

Baratang Island, in contrast to Havelock Island, is mostly used as a centre for natural sightseeing because it is less developed for urban tourism. The government ferry service connects Port Blair city at the Phoenix Bay Jetty with Baratang island. The Baratang Island is traversed by the Andaman Trunk Road, which runs between Rangat and Mayabunder and meanders through the verdant jungles.
